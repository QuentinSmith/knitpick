      Your body measurements
      
          
      Add your measurements once and they'll stay put. Each one shows what it comes to in stitches or rows at your gauge, and updates as you change needle or gauge.
      
        
      
      
      
      
      
      
      
        
        
      
      
      
      Measurements around the body, like a chest, become stitches; lengths up the body become rows.

      And a circumference is the whole way around — so for a piece knit flat, front and back separately, you'll work roughly half of it per panel, plus a little ease.
      
    
      
  
      

  
  
      
    
      
      
      
        
        
      
          
      Your setups
      
          
      Two little libraries to mix and match. Mechanical holds a needle and the gauge you got with it — load one to set your working gauge. Materials holds your yarns — pick one as the active yarn.
      
        
      
      
      

      
      
        
        
      
      
      
      
      
      
      
      Mechanical and Materials are kept separate, so one needle-and-gauge can pair with lots of different yarns.

      Just one thing to keep in mind: gauge really belongs to a yarn and needle together. Reusing a mechanical with another yarn assumes it knits up the same — fine within a weight (DK with DK), but for a very different yarn it's worth a fresh one.
